## Transparency Rules

1. Say what is known
2. Say what is not yet known
3. Say what happens next
4. Say who owns the next step when appropriate
5. Do not fill uncertainty with guesswork

## When Transparency Matters Most

1. Delays
2. Document requests
3. Status changes
4. Expectation setting
5. Relationship recovery after a missed follow up

## Recommended Message Pattern

1. Current status
2. Reason for the message
3. Confirmed next step
4. Timing, only if verified
5. Invitation for questions or confirmation

## Language To Prefer

1. Here is what we know right now
2. Here is the next step
3. I will update you when this changes
4. Here is what I need from you

## Language To Avoid

1. Empty reassurance with no next step
2. Certainty you cannot support
3. Passive phrasing that hides ownership
4. Jargon that masks the real issue

## Review Gate

Before reuse, confirm:

1. The message makes uncertainty visible without causing confusion
2. The owner and next step are clear
3. No unsupported claim slipped in
